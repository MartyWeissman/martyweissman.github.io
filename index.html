Mathematical Modeling and Simulations

By Martin H. Weissman

Welcome to my html/javascript simulations. These are meant as tools for students learning mathematical modeling in the life sciences.

Simulations

  • Caffeine simulator. Track the caffeine in your body, with three adjustable doses each day.
  • Shark and tuna interaction-counter. Relate number of interactions to populations of sharks and tuna.
  • Shark and tuna trajectories. Trajectories in state space and time-series. Interventions.
  • Insulin Glucose response. Set parameters for insulin sensitivty, beta cell mass, etc. Look at stead state or a glucose challenge test. Uses a minimal model.
  • Simulation of Gentamicin Dosage. Adjust dosage, frequency, infusion duration to explore effect on drug in bloodstream. Exhibits 1st order kinetics (exponential decay).
  • Self-interaction Simulator Relate the number of self-interactions to populations of moving organisms in a dish.

Tools

  • Dynamic vector field explorer. Play with your own vector fields in the plane, and watch particles blow in the wind.
  • Data plotter with log-scaling.Enter 10 data points, experiment with log-scaling and line of best fit.
  • Logistic growth explorer.Explore parameter changes, and try to fit logistic model to your data by minimizing the residual sum of squares.
  • Exploration of equilibrium and bifurcation. Shift a parameter, and choose inflow/outflow functions to explore 1-dimensional dynamical systems with equilibrium and bifurcations.
  • Generalized Lotka-Volterra explorer. Control parameters in a 2-dimensional generalized Lotka-Volterra system. Allows exploration of predator-prey, competition, cooperation, coupled with logistic growth.

Study aids

  • Fundamental modeling practice.Practice with randomly generated situations, figure out change equations, graphs, and more. Click buttons to see correct answers.

Data Sets

  • Tuna and sharks in Trieste. Real fisheries data from the port in Trieste. Kilograms of sharks and tuna caught each year.
